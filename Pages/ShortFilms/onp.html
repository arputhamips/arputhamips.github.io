Oru Nodi Pozhuthu unfolds against the backdrop of an unforgiving examination system—a crucible where young minds are forged into engineers, their futures hanging in precarious balance. Our protagonist, an earnest adolescent, grapples with the daunting task of mastering complex equations and theories. The exam looms like a storm cloud, threatening to drown him in despair.

But destiny has other plans. A mysterious time machine materializes, its metallic hum echoing through the corridors of possibility. Armed with the answers to his impending exam, our protagonist makes a daring choice: to rewrite his future. He steps into the machine, the seconds ticking like heartbeats.

The past unfurls before him—a canvas of pivotal moments. Armed with knowledge from the future, he revisits critical junctures, hoping to alter the course of events. Yet, time is not linear; it’s a tempest that bends and twists. Just as he prepares to change his fate, a stranger appears—a version of himself from an even more distant future. This older self carries a burden heavier than textbooks: regret. He has witnessed the consequences of meddling with time, and he’s determined to prevent his younger counterpart from making the same mistake.
